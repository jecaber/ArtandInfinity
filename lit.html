ART AND INFINITY

A Math 10 Group Project

Are you a fan of books?

In works like Virginia Woolf’s Orlando, the protagonist lives for centuries, witnessing the changing nature of time, gender, and identity. This invokes the infinite variability of experience over an endless timeline.

Like songs that fade instead of ending, some literary works never quite conclude, leaving the narrative unresolved or cyclical, suggesting that the story continues beyond the page—a literary invocation of infinity.
