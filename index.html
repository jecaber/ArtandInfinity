Painting

Johannes Vermeer’s Girl with a Pearl Earring subtly follows the golden ratio, with the composition unfolding in a golden spiral outward from the center of the painting. The girl’s posture and the placement of the painting’s elements align with this spiral, guiding the viewer’s eye and creating a natural sense of balance and harmony.

Infinite Mirror Rooms

Yayoi Kusama’s Infinity Mirror Rooms bring the idea of infinity to life through mirrors and repeating patterns that surround and reflect the viewer. First introduced in her 1965 work Infinity Mirror Room (Phalli’s Field), these installations allowed her to move beyond the physical limits of making each object by hand, using mirrors to multiply forms endlessly.
The experience is both visual and emotional as viewers see themselves reflected in a space that seems to go on forever, creating a feeling of being part of something much larger. With themes of repetition, self-loss, and connection to the universe, Kusama’s rooms offer a powerful and immersive way to explore the idea of infinity.
